## Design Context

### Users
31-year-old hybrid athlete (Hyrox competitor) in active body recomposition. Uses the app daily on mobile for quick meal logging, weekly on desktop for planning review. High competence, low patience for friction. They treat nutrition as a training variable, not a wellness habit. They have monthly InBody scans and want the data to mean something.

### Brand Personality
Precise. Uncompromising. Earned.

The product is a cockpit, not a wellness app. Think sports performance software — the kind an elite athlete would trust, not the kind a lifestyle brand would sell. No softness, no encouragement theater, no green smoothies. The tone is direct, the data is the hero, and every word earns its place.

Anti-references: MyFitnessPal (database UI masquerading as a tool), Noom (coaching theater), any app with a fruit icon or pastel macro rings.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Dark mode only.** Deep near-black (#0A0B0D), never pure black. Charts and numbers glow against it.
- **Single accent: chartreuse (#D4FF3A).** Used only for live state, primary actions, and emphasis. Absence makes it powerful.
- **Typography does the work.** Large display numbers are the hero. Space Grotesk for display, Inter for body — but the impeccable skill should find non-reflex alternatives that carry the same athletic-precision feel.
- **Density over decoration.** Whitespace is earned. No decorative icons, no illustrations, no gradients.
- **Motion is diagnostic.** Animations confirm computation (a number lands, a log is confirmed). Nothing moves for show.

### Design Principles
1. **Data as hero.** Numbers are larger than anything else on the screen. If the data isn't the most visually dominant element, the layout is wrong.
2. **Every word is load-bearing.** Labels, headlines, copy — nothing decorative. Cut until it hurts, then cut again.
3. **Friction is a design failure.** If something takes more than one tap or 10 seconds, the design needs to change.
4. **Earned restraint.** The chartreuse accent only appears where something is live, active, or needs action. Overuse kills it.
5. **Cockpit, not calendar.** The interface should feel like a performance instrument, not a planner or journal.
